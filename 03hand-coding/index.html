7 Common Chemical Allergies

Allergies are over-reactions from our immune systems. Most people are familiar with allergies related to food or the changes of the season, but not allergies to chemicals that create a reaction when they come in contact with skin.

"Allergic contact dermatitis -- happen when your immune system overreacts to chemicals that are normally harmless. They can be in products you're exposed to over and over, like cleaners, colognes, hair dyes, and personal care items.Even if you've used them before, you can still have a reaction."

Origin of Allergies

  • Allergies to certain allergens are more common than that of others.
  • It is not entirely understood where allergies come from, but we do know of several factors that can impact the likelihood of an indivual contracting them, including: environmental and genetic.
Allergen
A substance that can cause an allergic reaction in some people. Common allergens include pollen, pet dander, dust mites, mold, certain foods, and chemicals. When an allergen is encountered, it triggers the immune system to overreact, causing symptoms of allergies.
Allergies
A condition in which the immune system reacts abnormally to a foreign substance, such as pollen, food, or pet dander, that is harmless to most people. Symptoms of allergies can range from mild (like sneezing, itching, or rashes) to severe (such as difficulty breathing or anaphylaxis).
Contact Dermatisis
A skin condition caused by direct contact with an irritant or allergen. It results in red, itchy, and sometimes painful inflammation of the skin. Contact dermatitis can be either irritant (from exposure to chemicals or harsh substances) or allergic (when the immune system reacts to a specific substance).
